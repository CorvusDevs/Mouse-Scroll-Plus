macOS gives its trackpad beautifully smooth, momentum-based scrolling and leaves the mouse wheel jumping line by line. Mouse Scroll Plus brings that fluid feel to any mouse. A native **SwiftUI** menu-bar app built on a lightweight Core Graphics event tap, it turns every click of the wheel into a smooth glide, reverses direction, tunes speed and momentum, and puts the extra buttons your mouse wastes to work. Sandboxed, no tracking, everything runs on your Mac. A one-time purchase on the Mac App Store.

## Features

- **Smooth, fluid scrolling.** Turn each click of the mouse wheel into a gliding, trackpad-style motion instead of a stepped jump.
- **Reverse the wheel independently.** Flip vertical or horizontal scroll direction for the mouse while your trackpad keeps behaving normally.
- **Tune the feel.** Adjust scroll speed, smoothness, acceleration, and momentum until it matches your hand.
- **Momentum coasting.** A quick flick keeps gliding for a moment after you let go, just like a trackpad.
- **Remap extra buttons.** Bind the side and other extra mouse buttons to keyboard shortcuts, media keys, and editing commands. Left, right, and the scroll wheel are never changed.
- **Launch apps and run Shortcuts.** Map a button to open an app, open a URL, or run any macOS Shortcut.
- **Middle-button autoscroll.** Hold the middle button and move the mouse to scroll, Windows-style.
- **Per-modifier layers.** Give the same button a different action while you hold a modifier key.
- **Scroll the window under the pointer.** Scroll whatever window is beneath the cursor, even when it is not focused.
- **Stepped mode.** Prefer classic line-by-line scrolling? Set exactly how many lines each wheel click moves.
- **Hold to scroll normally.** Keep a key held for raw, precise scrolling whenever you need it.
- **Per-app rules.** Exclude the apps you choose, or limit the effect to only the ones you pick, and disable it per app in a click.
- **Global toggle and launch at login.** A system-wide shortcut turns every enhancement on or off from anywhere, and the app can start quietly at login.
- **Lives in the menu bar.** Every setting a click away, with no Dock clutter.
- **33 languages.** Fully localized with a built-in language picker, including right-to-left support for Arabic and Hebrew.
- **Built for privacy.** No account, no telemetry, no tracking. Runs entirely on your Mac.

## What makes Mouse Scroll Plus special

🖱️ **Any mouse feels like a trackpad.** The smooth, momentum-based scrolling macOS reserves for its own trackpad, brought to every wheel mouse, with the speed and feel tuned to you.

🎯 **Put the wasted buttons to work.** Side and extra buttons become shortcuts, media keys, app launchers, URLs, and Shortcuts, with a separate layer under each modifier. Left, right, and the wheel are always left untouched.

🎛️ **Tuned to your hand.** Speed, smoothness, acceleration, and momentum are all adjustable, plus a stepped line-by-line mode and hold-to-scroll-normally for precision work.

🧩 **Per-app control.** Keep the effect everywhere except a few apps, or only in the ones you choose, and flip it per app or globally with a shortcut.

🌐 **33 languages, fully translated.** Every string, menu, and onboarding step localized, with a one-click language picker and right-to-left layouts for Arabic and Hebrew.

🛡️ **Private and lightweight.** Sandboxed, no accounts, no telemetry, no analytics. The only permission it needs is Accessibility, and nothing ever leaves your Mac.

## Requirements

macOS 13 Ventura or later, on Apple Silicon or Intel. Mouse Scroll Plus reads and reshapes mouse-wheel and button events, which macOS allows only with **Accessibility** permission. Add the app once in System Settings and scrolling turns on immediately, with no restart.

## Built with

**SwiftUI** · **AppKit** · **Core Graphics** · **CGEventTap** · **App Sandbox**

## Privacy

No accounts, no analytics, no telemetry, no tracking. Mouse Scroll Plus runs entirely on your Mac and makes no network requests. It stores only your own settings, in the app's standard preferences on your device, and they never leave your Mac.
